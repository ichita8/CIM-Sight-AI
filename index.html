02 / Sample Output

What the Engine
Actually Surfaces.

Representative red flags from a live CIM analysis. Every output follows the same structured format — auditable, quotable, actionable.

Math Error EBITDA Margin Contradiction Across Sections
HIGH
"EBITDA of $42.3M on revenues of $187.5M represents a 22.6% margin, consistent with prior year performance of 22.8%."

$42.3M / $187.5M = 22.56%, but Page 34 shows FY2022 EBITDA of $39.1M on $171.2M revenue — that's 22.84%, not 22.8%. The rounding obscures a 24bps discrepancy. Minor on its own; a pattern across multiple pages indicates either sloppy modelling or intentional smoothing.

Aggressive Projections 18–22% CAGR With Zero Substantiation
HIGH
"Management projects revenue growth of 18–22% CAGR over the next five years, driven by geographic expansion and new product lines."

LTM growth was 9.4%. The prior year was 7.1%. A 2x acceleration requires signed LOIs, named markets, and pipeline disclosure — none of which appear in this document. "Geographic expansion" and "new product lines" are not a plan. This is a seller's fantasy dressed in CAGR notation.

Customer Concentration Top 3 Clients = 61% of Total Revenue
MEDIUM
"The Company benefits from long-standing relationships with its top clients, with its three largest customers representing approximately 61% of total revenue."

61% concentration in three clients is one contract cancellation away from a covenant breach. The word "approximately" is doing a lot of work here. We need exact figures and contract expiry dates — if any are up for renewal within 18 months of close, this deal reprices materially.

Management Language Recurring "One-Time" Cost Classification
MEDIUM
"Margins are expected to normalize over the projection period as one-time integration costs from recent acquisitions are absorbed by the business."

"Normalize" and "one-time costs" appearing together is a textbook tell. Every year these are one-time. If integration costs recur across multiple acquisitions, they're structural — not exceptional. Request a 5-year history of items management has classified as non-recurring. The answer will be illuminating.
